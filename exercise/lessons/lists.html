Links, Lists and Tables (Part 2)

This is a multipart exercise that will deal with working on the same piece of HTML code. As always, just follow the instructions to complete this exercise.

Lists in HTML are just like lists in real life. We're going to define an "li" (list item) before we define the list as a whole.

Step 1

Around your newly created link(s), add your beginning and ending "li" tags. If you have more than one link, add additional "li" tags around those as well.

Step 2

Wrap your list item(s) with "ul" (unordered list) tags. This will create a list with generic bullet points next to each of your list items.

Step 3

Copy your entire unordered list. And paste the copy below that list.

Change your "ul" tags in this copied list to "ol" (ordered list) tags. The HTML term "ordered list" is a bit of a misnomer as it won't sort the list for you but will change the bullet points from generic dots to numbers.

Step 4

If you haven't already, add some additional links (inside list items) to your lists.

Once you've completed these steps, refresh the web page.
